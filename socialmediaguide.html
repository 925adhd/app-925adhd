ADHD & Social Media: The Real Talk
Why this can be amazing or terrible for you
3 min ‚ñº

‚úÖ ADHD Advantages

  • Creativity bursts: Great for content ideation during hyperfocus
  • Authenticity: ADHD brains often come across as genuine and relatable
  • Trend-spotting: Novelty-seeking = early trend detection
  • Passion: When you care about a topic, it shows

‚ùå ADHD Danger Zones

  • Doom-scrolling: Platforms are designed to trap your attention
  • Comparison trap: Seeing others' success can tank motivation
  • Consistency struggle: Algorithms reward daily posting
  • Dopamine addiction: Likes and comments = intermittent reward
  • Burnout: The "always on" pressure is real

‚ö†Ô∏è Honest Assessment

Social media creation can be ADHD-friendly as a hobby or side project. As a job? The consistency requirements often clash with ADHD's variable energy and focus.

My take: Build it slowly on the side. Don't quit your other income sources.

ADHD-Friendly Strategies
How to actually make this sustainable
4 min ‚ñº

üì¶ Batch Creating (The ADHD Hack)

  1. Wait for hyperfocus to hit
  2. Create 5-10 pieces of content in one session
  3. Schedule them out over weeks
  4. Repeat when energy returns

Tools: Later, Buffer, or platform-native scheduling

üéØ Energy Matching

  • Hyperfocus: Film, write scripts, batch create
  • Medium energy: Edit, respond to comments, engage
  • Low energy: Consume content for research, save ideas
  • No energy: Don't force it. Rest is productive.

üõ°Ô∏è Protect Your Brain

  • Time limits: Set app timers (seriously)
  • Separate accounts: Creator vs. consumer profiles
  • Scheduled posting: Post and leave
  • Comment batching: Check once daily, not constantly

üí∞ Realistic Income Strategy

Instead of chasing followers, focus on:

  • Affiliate links: Earn from products you mention
  • Digital products: Sell templates, guides, presets
  • Services: Use content to attract clients
  • Sponsorships: Reach out directly to brands you use

1,000 engaged followers > 100,000 passive ones
